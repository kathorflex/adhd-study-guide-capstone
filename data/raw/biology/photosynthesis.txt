Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants, algae, and some bacteria turn sunlight into chemical energy stored in glucose.  The reaction takes place in chloroplasts, specialized organelles that contain the pigment chlorophyll.  Light energy is first captured during the light‑dependent reactions, producing ATP and NADPH.  In the second stage, the Calvin cycle, carbon dioxide from the atmosphere is fixed into a sugar molecule using the ATP and NADPH generated earlier.  The overall simplified equation is: 6 CO₂ + 6 H₂O + light → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6 O₂.  This process supplies most of the oxygen in Earth’s atmosphere and forms the base of most food webs.
