Newton’s second law of motion states that the force acting on an object equals the mass of the object multiplied by its acceleration (F = m·a).  This law explains how an object’s speed changes when a net force is applied.  If the same force pushes a lighter object, the lighter object accelerates more than a heavier one.  The same principle applies to rotational motion, where torque equals the moment of inertia times angular acceleration (τ = I·α).  Newton’s second law is a cornerstone of classical mechanics and appears in everyday situations, from pushing a shopping cart to calculating the thrust needed for a rocket launch.
