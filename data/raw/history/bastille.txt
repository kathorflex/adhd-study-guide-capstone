On 14 July 1789, a crowd of Parisians stormed the Bastille, a medieval fortress prison that symbolised royal tyranny. The crowd was motivated by a price‑of‑bread shortage, political agitation, and rumors that the king was amassing weapons. Around 600 men entered the prison, freed the few prisoners inside, and seized the stored gunpowder. The fall of the Bastille quickly became a rallying point for the French Revolution, marking the collapse of absolute monarchy and the rise of a citizen‑based government.

On 14 July 1789 Parisians attacked the Bastille, a large prison that represented the king’s power. They were angry about a lack of bread, political ideas, and rumors that the king kept weapons there. Around six hundred people entered, released the few prisoners, and took the gunpowder stored inside. The fall of the Bastille became a famous moment that showed the power of ordinary citizens and sparked the French Revolution.
