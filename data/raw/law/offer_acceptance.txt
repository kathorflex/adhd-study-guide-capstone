A contract is formed when one party makes an offer and the other party accepts it. An offer must be communicated to the offeree, contain a clear intention to be bound, and specify the essential terms of the agreement. Acceptance must be an unequivocal expression of assent to the terms of the offer and must be communicated in a manner prescribed by the offeror, or, if none is prescribed, in a reasonable manner. The “mailbox rule” states that an acceptance is effective when it is placed in the mail, not when it is received. If the offeree proposes a counter‑offer, the original offer is terminated and a new offer is created.A contract requires an offer and an acceptance.||
