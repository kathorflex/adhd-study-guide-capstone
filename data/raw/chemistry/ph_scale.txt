The pH scale is a numeric representation of how acidic or basic a solution is.  The scale runs from 0 to 14; a pH lower than 7 indicates an acidic solution, a pH of 7 is neutral (pure water), and a pH higher than 7 indicates a basic (alkaline) solution.  The pH value is defined as the negative base‑10 logarithm of the hydrogen‑ion concentration: pH = –log10[H⁺].  Strong acids, such as hydrochloric acid, dissociate completely in water and produce very low pH values close to 0.  Strong bases, like sodium hydroxide, dissociate completely and give pH values close to 14. Most biological systems keep pH within a narrow range; for example, human blood stays around pH 7.4 thanks to buffering systems that resist large changes.
