Binary is the simplest number system a computer can use, consisting of only two digits: 0 and 1.  Every piece of digital information—text, images, sound—is ultimately stored as a long string of these two symbols.  A group of eight bits is called a byte, which can represent any integer from 0 to 255.  To calculate the value of a binary number, you add up the powers of 2 for each position that contains a 1.  For example, the binary number 101₂ means 1·2² + 0·2¹ + 1·2⁰ = 5 in decimal.  This “on/off” representation matches the physical reality of electronic circuits, where a voltage is either present (1) or absent (0).
